## Vitamin D — 8.77 ng/mL

**Severity:** Severe deficiency. This level is associated with:

- Reduced muscle strength and recovery
- Lower testosterone production
- Fatigue, joint discomfort, impaired immunity
- Poor calcium absorption (muscle function, bone health)

**Your current protocol (correct):**

- **60,000 IU D3 once weekly** — standard Indian loading dose
- Take with a **fat-containing meal** (eggs, ghee, fish oil)
- Add **Vitamin K2 (MK-7) 90–120 mcg** on D3 day

**3-month plan:**

| Phase | Weeks | D3 Protocol |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| Loading | 1–8 | 60,000 IU **every week** |
| Reassess | Week 8–12 | Continue weekly **unless** doctor adjusts after mid-retest |
| Retest | Week 12 | Target: **≥40 ng/mL** |

**Do not** take 60,000 IU daily — weekly only.

**Sun:** 15–20 min midday sun on arms/face when possible (India — still often insufficient alone at 8.77).

## Vitamin B12 — 237 pg/mL

**Severity:** Below functional optimal. Lab may flag as "normal" (>200), but for athletic recovery and neurological health, **400–500+ pg/mL** is a better target.

**Why you're low despite eating eggs/chicken:**

- Red meat only **1–2×/month** — not enough to maintain B12 stores
- Eggs/chicken provide modest B12 vs mutton/beef/liver
- Heavy training increases B12 demand

**Protocol:**

- **Methylcobalamin 500 mcg/day** — continue (consider **1000 mcg** if still <400 at retest)
- Take **morning, empty stomach** — 30 min before food
- **Do not** rely on diet alone during this bulk

**Retest target:** **>400 pg/mL** at Week 12
